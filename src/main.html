TRACKS OVER TIME

Our public transport history

The efficiency of Singapore’s public transport system primarily emerged from the need to allow large number of people to travel within the limited land space. Singapore started with buses as the primary mode of public transport in the 1950s. The rail system was proposed and launched in 1980s to serve the rapidly growing population and ease traffic congestion.

In May 2019, the Land Transport Authority released the Land Transport Master Plan 2040 that aims to transform the nation into a 45 minute city and 20 minute town by 2040.

JORUNEY THROUGH DECADES

The evolution of the public transport system

1960s

A 1963 report from the United Nations Development Program (UNDP) suggested Singapore should invest in monorail or subway systems as it will suit the city landscape and is a cost-effective option.

1970s

The Urban Redevelopment Authority (URA) proposed a concept plan in 1971 which included a rail system to serve as connections between the city center and satellite towns.

2000s

By the early 2000s, Singapore’s urban public transport infrastructure has transformed and became a pioneering example of a ‘transport metropolis’ in the world.

THE ROAD AHEAD

What's next for public transport

Land Transport Master Plan 2040 charts Singapore's long-term vision and policies that envisions a transport system that is convenient, well-connected and fast. The 3 key pillars include:

  • 20-min Town & 45-min City
  • Transport for All
  • Healthy Lives, Safer Journey
